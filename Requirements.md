- Icons, illustrations, and screenshots embedded in copy—not floating elements

- Primary: Clean, focused (likely a vibrant accent for CTAs and highlights)
- Neutral: Light backgrounds with dark text (high contrast for readability)
- Streaks/Progress: Warm color (flame/gold) for gamification visuals
- TBD: Finalize during Phase 3 (Branding)

**Typography:**
- Headlines: Bold, large, outcome-focused language
- Body: Clear, readable sans-serif, generous line height
- CTA text: Action-oriented, conversational ("Start your 7-day free trial")
- TBD: Font family + sizing finalized in Phase 3

**Component Style:**
- Buttons: Prominent, rounded, high contrast (encourage clicks)
- Cards: Subtle borders/shadows (not heavy)
- Screenshots: Integrated into copy flow, labeled with step numbers
- Icons: Minimal, support narrative (not decorative)

**Design Tone:**
- **Friendly, not corporate.** Voice matches copy tone.
- **Modern native Mac design.** Align with system-level app aesthetic (clean, minimal, functional).
- **Proof-focused.** Screenshots, diagrams, and data visualization build credibility.
- **Dark mode consideration:** Design should work on both light + dark backgrounds.

## Research & Credibility Anchors

### Lost Focus Problem (Context Switching Cost)
**The Stat:** 23 minutes 15 seconds to fully refocus after one interruption  
**Research Source:** [UC Irvine study](https://www.iq-it.co.uk/blog/hidden-cost-of-context-switching)  
**Supporting Data:**
- Knowledge workers spend <3 minutes on a task before switching (Microsoft)
- Average ~275 interruptions per workday
- Context switching = 40% of productive time (3.2 hours lost per 8-hour day)
- Heavy multitasking causes temporary 10-point IQ drop (worse than losing sleep)

**Landing Page Use:** "One interruption costs you 23 minutes" ✓ Citable, visceral

---

### Why Sprints Work (Short Bursts + Recovery)
**Key Research:** [Pomodoro/focus sprint effectiveness studies](https://www.float.com/resources/what-is-the-pomodoro-technique)  
**Correlation Data:**
- Focus & concentration: +72% correlation
- Student performance: +65% correlation  
- Learning engagement: +68% correlation
- Time-structured sprints outperform self-paced breaks on sustained performance

**Why NOT to Say "Pomodoro":**
- Avoid being labeled "just another timer"
- Frame as "sprint + recovery science" backed by "50+ years of focus research"
- Emphasize guided experience: "we handle the timing, you focus on work"

### Why Measurement + Gamification Works (Loss Aversion)
**Measurement Impact:** +12% productivity increase via tracking (WHO research)  
**Loss Aversion Psychology:** [Dr. Katy Milkman, U Penn behavioral scientist](https://www.psychologicalscience.org/news/to-build-a-habit-try-a-streak.html)
- Losses feel ~2x more powerful than gains
- Streaks leverage this: fear of breaking the streak > motivation to start fresh
- Longer the streak, the more users feel they have to lose

**Landing Page Use:** "What you measure improves. Your streak keeps you honest."
